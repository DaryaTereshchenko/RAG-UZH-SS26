# Criteria

## Overview

This policy classifies content that applies harmful stereotypes to individuals or groups based on protected attributes.

## Definition of Terms

- Protected Attributes: Race, ethnicity, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, caste, immigration status, national origin, serious disease, sex, tribe, gender identity.
- Stereotyping: Applying generalized, typically negative assumptions about an entire group based on protected attributes, including attributing specific behaviors, traits, or roles to all members of that group.
- Harmful Generalization: Broad statements that ascribe negative characteristics (criminality, immorality, inferiority, incompetence, dangerousness) to an entire protected group.

## Interpretation of Language

- Content that frames negative generalizations about a protected group as factual, statistical, or observational claims still qualifies if it reinforces harmful stereotypes.
- Assigning fixed social roles, behaviors, or capabilities to a group based on protected attributes qualifies even when presented as tradition, religion, or common sense.
- Content that uses qualifiers like "most," "a lot of," or "in general" when attributing negative traits to a protected group still qualifies as stereotyping.
- Comparisons between protected groups that portray one group as inherently superior or inferior qualify.

## Definition of Labels

### (ST): Harmful Stereotyping

#### Includes

- Attributing criminality, violence, or dangerous behavior to an entire protected group
- Portraying a protected group as inherently inferior, incompetent, or less capable
- Assigning rigid gender roles or expectations based on gender, sex, or gender identity
- Generalizing negative cultural, moral, or behavioral traits to an entire racial, ethnic, religious, or national group
- Framing immigration status, national origin, or ethnicity as predictive of criminal or antisocial behavior
- Using statistics or anecdotes to reinforce negative generalizations about a protected group

#### Excludes

- Unedited movie or TV clips
- Content using offensive language in clearly satirical or comedic contexts without genuine harmful intent
- Content criticizing people in positions of significant public power (politicians, police, public officials) focusing on their actions or policies rather than targeting protected attributes
- Content discussing stereotypes in an educational or analytical context with appropriate framing that does not reinforce them
- Content using potentially offensive language where the speaker is a member of the same group being referenced
- Content that may be problematic but does not attribute traits or behaviors to groups based on protected attributes as defined above
