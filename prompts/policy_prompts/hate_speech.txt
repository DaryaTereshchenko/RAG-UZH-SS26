# Criteria

## Overview

This policy classifies content that constitutes hate speech or hateful behaviors directed at people based on protected attributes.

## Definition of Terms

- Protected Attributes: Race, ethnicity, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, caste, immigration status, national origin, serious disease, sex, tribe, gender identity.
- Hate Speech: Language that attacks, demeans, or incites violence or discrimination against individuals or groups based on their protected attributes.
- Hateful Ideologies: Belief systems that promote the superiority or inferiority of people based on protected attributes, including white supremacy, misogyny, anti-LGBTQ+ hate, antisemitism, and Islamophobia.
- Dehumanization: Describing or portraying members of a protected group as subhuman, animalistic, or inherently inferior.

## Interpretation of Language

- Content that frames negative generalizations about a protected group as factual or statistical claims still qualifies if it demeans or promotes discrimination.
- Content that uses coded language or dogwhistles targeting protected groups qualifies if the intent to demean or incite is clear from context.

## Definition of Labels

### (HS): Hate Speech and Hateful Behaviors

#### Includes

- Encouraging violence, segregation, discrimination, or other harm towards people based on protected attributes
- Demeaning someone on the basis of protected attributes including dehumanizing people, portraying protected groups as inherently dangerous, using hateful slurs, deadnaming or misgendering, promoting conversion therapy
- Denying or minimizing well-documented historical events that harmed protected groups (Holocaust, Rwandan genocide)
- Supporting or spreading hateful ideologies including white supremacy, misogyny, anti-LGBTQ+ hate, antisemitism, Islamophobia, or hateful conspiracies
- Displaying symbols and imagery associated with hateful movements

#### Excludes

- Unedited movie or TV clips
- Content using offensive language in clearly satirical or comedic contexts without genuine harmful intent
- Content criticizing people in positions of significant public power (politicians, police, public officials) focusing on their actions or policies rather than targeting protected attributes
- Content using potentially offensive language where the speaker is a member of the same group being referenced
- Educational or documentary content with appropriate framing that does not promote harmful behavior
- Content that may be problematic but does not target people based on protected attributes as defined above
