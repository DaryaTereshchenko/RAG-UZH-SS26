# Criteria

## Overview

This policy classifies content that shows, promotes, or threatens physical violence.

## Definition of Terms

- Physical Violence: The use of physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill someone.
- Violent Threats: Statements communicating an intent to inflict physical harm on a person, whether direct, conditional, or implied.
- Graphic Violence: Detailed depictions of physical injury, torture, or death.
- Violent Organizations: Groups that use or promote violence to achieve their goals, including violent extremists, terrorist organizations, and criminal organizations.

## Interpretation of Language

- Threats that are conditional (e.g., "if you do X, I will hurt you") still qualify as violent threats.
- Implied threats communicated through context or intimidation qualify even without explicit language.
- Glorification or celebration of real-world violence qualifies even without direct threats.
- Hypothetical or rhetorical statements advocating for violence in general terms qualify if they promote or normalize physical violence.

## Definition of Labels

### (VT): Physical Violence and Violent Threats

#### Includes

- Showing, promoting, or threatening physical violence including real-world torture, graphic violence, and extreme physical fighting
- Promoting or materially supporting violent or hateful organizations, including violent extremists and criminal organizations
- Direct threats to physically harm, injure, or kill someone
- Glorifying, celebrating, or inciting acts of physical violence

#### Excludes

- Unedited movie or TV clips
- Content using offensive language in clearly satirical or comedic contexts without genuine harmful intent
- Content criticizing people in positions of significant public power (politicians, police, public officials) focusing on their actions or policies rather than targeting protected attributes
- Educational or documentary content with appropriate framing that does not promote harmful behavior
- Content that may be aggressive or confrontational but does not show, promote, or threaten physical violence as defined above
