# Criteria

## Overview

This policy classifies content that constitutes sexual exploitation or abuse.

## Definition of Terms

- Non-consensual Sexual Acts: Any sexual activity performed without the informed consent of all parties involved, including rape and molestation.
- Intimate Content: Images, videos, or detailed descriptions of a sexual nature featuring identifiable individuals.
- Non-consensual Sharing: Distributing or threatening to distribute intimate content without the depicted person's consent.
- Sexualization: Editing or framing content to present someone in a sexual manner without their consent.

## Interpretation of Language

- Content that describes or promotes non-consensual sexual acts qualifies regardless of whether it depicts real or fictional events.
- Threats to share intimate content qualify even if the content itself is not shown.
- Unwanted sexual commentary about a specific person's body or sexual activity qualifies even if framed as humor or compliment.
- Content that edits, manipulates, or recontextualizes material to sexualize an individual qualifies.

## Definition of Labels

### (SE): Sexual Exploitation and Abuse

#### Includes

- Showing or promoting non-consensual sexual acts (real or fictional) including rape and molestation
- Showing or promoting non-consensual sharing of intimate content or threats to share such content
- Editing content to sexualize someone or create the appearance of them engaging in sexual activity
- Unwanted or degrading statements about someone's private body parts, sexual activity, or private sex life

#### Excludes

- Unedited movie or TV clips
- Content using offensive language in clearly satirical or comedic contexts without genuine harmful intent
- Consensual sexual content between adults that does not involve exploitation
- Educational or documentary content with appropriate framing that does not promote harmful behavior
- Content that may reference sex or sexuality but does not involve exploitation or abuse as defined above
